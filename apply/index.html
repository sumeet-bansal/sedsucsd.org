Fall 2017 Engineering Openings
Aurora​ is a vertical take-off, vertical landing reusable vehicle, currently in its pre-design stage,and is the newest challenge SEDS UCSD is undertaking. For Fall 2017, the Aurora team will be split up into two: a structural and R&D team.

Aurora Structural and Propellant Feed Members

Design and manufacture the body of the Aurora vehicle. A small avionics team will also be recruited for the project to setup the frameworks.

Needs:

  • Mechanically-oriented Members
  • Avionics Members

Aurora R&D team members

Small R& task force will take on multiple small-scale technology development projects that are designed to answer many crucial questions about a VTVL system.

Needs:

  • With a team, develop an RCS thruster system, a gimballing control box, and a telemetry system, and run rigid body simulations for the entire flight.
  • Needs:
    • Mechanical
    • Propellant Feed
    • Electronics
    • Software

Propulsion​ ​Team​ is team that develops our liquid propellant engines. Recruits will work on the design, manufacturing, and testing phases.

Propulsion Hardware Engineers

Research and design in essential hardware components such as ignition systems, data acquisition units, and structural hardware for liquid propellant rocket engines.

Qualifications:

  • 2nd year or above

Fluids Analysts

SAnalyze and verify engine design proposals using an engineering simulation software, such as ANSYS for Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFDs) and Finite Element Analysis (FEA).

Qualifications:

  • Classes (preferred): Thermodynamics, Fluid Dynamics, Solid Mechanics
  • 2nd year +, preferred 3rd year

Colossus​ is a trailer mounted static fire system designed to test rocket engines up to 3000 lbs in force and provide accurate information about the engine's performance. The system logs data, including thrust, internal pressures, and temperatures.

Electrical Engineers

Assemble and verify the function of a $30,000 electronics panel.
